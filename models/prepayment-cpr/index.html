Negative Convexity & Duration

Mortgage-backed securities exhibit negative convexity because homeowners prepay when rates fall. As rates drop, expected cash flows shorten (homeowners refinance), so the MBS price rises less than a comparable non-callable bond.

Conversely, when rates rise, prepayments slow and the MBS extends in duration -- it falls more in price than expected. This asymmetry means MBS investors are short an option to the homeowner.
